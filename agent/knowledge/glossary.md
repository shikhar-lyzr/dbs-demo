# Indian Banking Glossary

Short, plain-language definitions. No specific numbers — defer to DBS pages
for anything quantitative.

- **APR (Annual Percentage Rate):** The yearly cost of a loan including
  interest and mandatory fees, expressed as a percentage.
- **EMI (Equated Monthly Installment):** A fixed monthly payment on a loan
  that covers both interest and principal.
- **KYC (Know Your Customer):** Identity verification the bank is legally
  required to do before opening an account. Usually needs a government ID
  and proof of address.
- **IFSC (Indian Financial System Code):** An 11-character code identifying
  a specific bank branch for NEFT/RTGS/IMPS transfers.
- **IMPS (Immediate Payment Service):** Instant interbank transfer, works
  24x7, routed through NPCI.
- **NEFT (National Electronic Funds Transfer):** Interbank transfer
  settled in batches by the RBI. Works 24x7.
- **RTGS (Real-Time Gross Settlement):** Real-time interbank transfer
  typically used for larger amounts.
- **UPI (Unified Payments Interface):** Instant mobile-first payment system
  using a VPA (e.g. name@dbs) instead of account numbers.
- **MMID (Mobile Money Identifier):** A 7-digit code used for IMPS
  transfers where only a mobile number and MMID are known.
- **IPIN (Internet Personal Identification Number):** The password used to
  log in to digibank online banking.
- **CVV (Card Verification Value):** The 3-digit code on the back of your
  card. Never share it — DBS will never ask for it.
- **Virtual card number:** A temporary card number generated for safer
  online payments; the real card number stays hidden.
- **Statement balance:** The amount owed on a credit card as of the last
  billing cycle close.
- **Available balance:** Money in an account you can spend right now, after
  pending transactions and holds.
- **Minimum due:** The smallest amount payable on a credit card bill to
  avoid a late fee. Paying only this keeps interest running on the rest.
- **Spending limit:** The cap on how much can be spent on a card or
  through a channel (e.g. daily UPI limit) before the bank blocks further
  transactions.
