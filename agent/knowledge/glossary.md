# Indian Banking Glossary

Plain-language definitions for terms users encounter on dbs.com/in and in
digibank. No specific numbers — defer to DBS pages for anything
quantitative.

## Loans and interest

- **APR (Annual Percentage Rate):** The yearly cost of a loan, including
  interest and mandatory fees, expressed as a single percentage. Lets you
  compare loans on a like-for-like basis.
- **EMI (Equated Monthly Installment):** A fixed monthly payment on a loan
  that covers both interest and a part of the principal. EMIs stay level;
  the interest-vs-principal split shifts over time.
- **Principal:** The amount you actually borrowed, separate from the
  interest charged on it.
- **Tenure:** The length of the loan — how many months or years you'll be
  paying EMIs for.

## KYC and identity

- **KYC (Know Your Customer):** Identity verification the bank is legally
  required to do before opening an account, under RBI rules. Typically
  needs a government ID plus proof of address.
- **PAN (Permanent Account Number):** A 10-character alphanumeric income
  tax ID issued by the Indian government. Needed for most banking
  relationships above basic thresholds.
- **Aadhaar:** A 12-digit unique ID issued by UIDAI, widely accepted for
  KYC and address proof. Share only the last four digits in casual
  contexts; the full number is sensitive.
- **Form 60:** A declaration customers without a PAN submit in place of
  it for specified banking transactions.

## Cards

- **Credit limit:** The maximum amount you can owe on a credit card at
  any time.
- **Minimum payment (minimum due):** The smallest amount payable on a
  credit card statement to avoid a late fee. Paying only this keeps
  interest accruing on the rest.
- **Available balance:** Money in an account you can spend right now,
  after pending transactions and holds.
- **Statement balance:** The amount owed on a credit card as of the last
  billing cycle close.

## Transfers and payments

- **IFSC (Indian Financial System Code):** An 11-character code
  identifying a specific bank branch for NEFT, RTGS, and IMPS transfers.
- **MMID (Mobile Money Identifier):** A 7-digit code used for IMPS
  transfers when only a mobile number and MMID are known.
- **UPI (Unified Payments Interface):** Instant mobile-first payment
  system using a VPA (e.g. name@dbs) instead of account numbers. Works
  24x7.
- **NEFT (National Electronic Funds Transfer):** Interbank transfer
  settled by the RBI. Now available 24x7.
- **RTGS (Real-Time Gross Settlement):** Real-time interbank transfer,
  typically used for larger amounts. Available 24x7.
- **IMPS (Immediate Payment Service):** Instant interbank transfer
  routed through NPCI, available 24x7.

## Tax and credit

- **TDS on interest:** Tax Deducted at Source — the bank withholds tax
  on interest earned (e.g. on fixed deposits) above specified thresholds
  and remits it to the income tax department. You'll see it in Form
  26AS.
- **CIBIL score:** A three-digit credit score from TransUnion CIBIL
  summarising your credit history. Lenders use it when evaluating loan
  and card applications. Higher is better.
