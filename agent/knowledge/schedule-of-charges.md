# Schedule of Charges / Rates and Tariffs

Help for users on the "Schedule of Charges" or "Rates and Tariffs" page on
dbs.com/in. This is the source of truth for every fee DBS India charges.

## What the page contains

The Schedule of Charges is a comprehensive list of fees, organised by
product. Typical categories:

- **Savings and Current Accounts:** Non-maintenance of minimum balance
  charges, cash deposit / withdrawal limits beyond free counts, cheque
  return and cheque book charges, duplicate statement fees.
- **Debit Cards:** Annual fee, replacement fee, ATM transactions beyond
  the free monthly limit, international ATM usage fees.
- **Credit Cards:** Annual fee, late payment fee, over-limit fee,
  foreign currency markup, cash advance fee, EMI conversion charges.
- **Loans:** Processing fees, prepayment / foreclosure charges, EMI
  bounce fees, documentation charges.
- **Remittances and transfers:** NEFT, RTGS, IMPS, foreign currency
  remittance charges (outward and inward), demand draft issuance.
- **Miscellaneous:** Signature verification, balance confirmation,
  duplicate passbook, no-dues certificate.

## Format

Usually a PDF download or a long tabular web page. Organised by product
category first, then by specific charge. Some product variants (e.g.
different savings tiers) have their own sections — tell the user to pick
the row matching their specific account type.

## Rules for answering

- **Never quote a specific number.** Even if the user insists, the right
  answer is "The exact figure for your account type is on the Schedule of
  Charges — I can point you to the right section." Fees change, and
  account variants have different tiers.
- **GST applies on top.** Call this out when relevant: "Whatever fee is
  listed, GST (currently 18%) is added on top." Don't quote the GST
  figure as if it's DBS's — it's a statutory rate, and still worth the
  user confirming on the page.
- **For a specific transaction the user is worried about** (e.g. "I was
  charged X, is that right?"): don't validate or dispute the number.
  Point them to the relevant Schedule of Charges row and, if they think
  it's wrong, the Lodge a Complaint flow.

## Helping the user navigate

If the user is on the Schedule of Charges page and can't find something:

1. Confirm which product they're looking for (savings variant, card
   type, loan product).
2. Point them to the section heading that matches.
3. If the page is a PDF, suggest using Ctrl-F / Cmd-F with a keyword
   like "minimum balance" or "ATM".
4. If they still can't find it, suggest calling 24x7 phone banking
   (number on the back of their debit card or on the Contact Us page).
