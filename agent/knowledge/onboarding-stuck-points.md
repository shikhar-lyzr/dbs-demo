# Onboarding Stuck-Points — Q&A by Step

Common questions users ask while going through the DBS digiSavings account opening flow. Each answer includes the step it applies to, is kept to 2–4 sentences, and never quotes a number that isn't in the product brief.

---

## Step 1 — Digibank Login Screen

**Q: What is my Username?**
Your Username is the one you chose during account registration (Step 6 of the onboarding flow). It is not your Customer ID or mobile number — it is the unique name you created at the "Set username & password" step. If you registered but forgot your username, use the "Trouble logging in?" link on the login screen.

**Q: I can't log in / Trouble logging in**
Tap the "Trouble logging in?" link on the login screen — it will guide you through username or password recovery. If you haven't set a username yet, you haven't completed Step 6; start a fresh application or check if you received a save-confirmation earlier.

**Q: Is Username the same as User ID?**
Yes — Username and User ID refer to the same thing in the digibank app. It is the alphanumeric name you set during registration, not your mobile number or PAN.

## Step 2 — Welcome Carousel

**Q: Do I need PAN and Aadhaar to open an account?**
The fully-digital flow requires both PAN and Aadhaar. The welcome screen states: *"If you wish to open an account without PAN and Aadhaar or with CKYC, or do not wish to avail debit card, please visit your nearest DBS branch."*

**Q: What if I don't want a debit card?**
If you don't want a debit card, you'll need to visit a DBS branch to open the account — the app's digital flow is designed for users who will receive a debit card. The welcome carousel fine print confirms this.

**Q: Is this service available in my city?**
The digital onboarding is available in select cities only, as noted on the welcome screen. If your city or pincode isn't supported, the app will prompt you; in that case, visit your nearest DBS branch.

## Step 3 — Home / Link-or-Apply Hub

**Q: I have an LVB account — what do I do?**
Lakshmi Vilas Bank (LVB) merged into DBS Bank India in November 2020. If you have an existing LVB account, tap **LINK ACCOUNT** to connect it to digibank. For questions about your LVB account beyond what the app handles, contact DBS customer care.

**Q: Should I link an existing account or open a new one?**
Tap **LINK ACCOUNT** if you already have an LVB or DBS savings account and just want to access it through digibank. Tap **OPEN A SAVINGS ACCOUNT** if you are opening a brand-new account with DBS.

**Q: Which account should I open here?**
On this screen you're choosing the *product*, not a specific account tier — only **OPEN A SAVINGS ACCOUNT** is available for new customers. Tap that to begin the savings account application. You'll pick a specific tier (digiSavings, Growth Plus, or Growth Max) later in the flow once your basic details and KYC are done.

**Q: What's the difference between LINK ACCOUNT and OPEN A SAVINGS ACCOUNT?**
**LINK ACCOUNT** is for users who already have an LVB or DBS account and want to see it inside digibank. **OPEN A SAVINGS ACCOUNT** starts a brand-new savings account application for users who don't bank with DBS yet.

## Step 4 — "Tell us a bit about yourself"

**Q: My name doesn't match what's on my PAN**
The app asks for your name exactly as it appears on your PAN card. If there's a discrepancy between what you enter and what UIDAI/PAN records hold, the eKYC process may fail later. Correct any typos here and ensure it matches your PAN exactly.

**Q: My date of birth on my PAN is wrong**
The app uses your PAN-linked date of birth for verification. If the DOB on your PAN is incorrect, you'll need to correct it with the Income Tax Department before continuing the digital flow. Until then, a branch visit using your corrected identity documents may be the only option.

## Step 5 — "How do we reach you?"

**Q: Do I need to use a specific mobile number?**
Yes — you should use the mobile number linked to your Aadhaar account. The OTP at Step 9 is sent to the number registered with UIDAI for Aadhaar authentication. If you use a different number, the OTP-based eKYC will not work.

**Q: Is the Referral Code required?**
No. The Referral Code field is optional. You can leave it blank if you don't have one and tap NEXT.

## Step 6 — "Set username & password"

**Q: Can I save my progress and continue later?**
Yes. The app screen states: *"Your application is automatically saved once you've set a username and password; simply log in to resume anytime."* After completing Step 6 (SUBMIT), your progress is saved and you can return via the digibank login screen (Step 1) at any time.

**Q: What are the Username rules?**
The specific rules (allowed characters, length) are shown in the app at this step. The brief does not list them here — follow the in-app guidance.

**Q: I forgot my password while mid-application**
If you've already set your username and password (completed Step 6), use the "Trouble logging in?" link on the login screen to reset your password and resume your application. If you haven't reached Step 6 yet, you'll need to restart the application.

## Step 7 — "Enter your PAN details"

**Q: My name on PAN doesn't match my Aadhaar name**
The app's consent at Step 8 includes an indemnity clause: *"I indemnify DBS against all losses arising due to difference in my name on Aadhaar and PAN card, if any."* You can still proceed by agreeing to this, but a significant mismatch may cause issues during KYC verification. Consider correcting your records with UIDAI or the Income Tax Department first.

**Q: How do I link my PAN with Aadhaar?**
Tap the "How to link PAN with Aadhaar" help link shown on this screen — it will guide you through the process. This step is required for a successful eKYC.

**Q: I'm a US citizen or Green card holder — what do I do?**
On the PAN screen, tap the tax-residency option that says *"If you are a tax resident of one or more countries other than India, which includes being US citizen/Green card holder, tap here."* This routes you through a different declaration path appropriate for your tax status.

**Q: I don't have a PAN number**
Tap **DON'T HAVE PAN NUMBER**. As noted on the welcome screen, opening an account without PAN requires a branch visit — the fully-digital flow is not available without PAN.

## Step 8 — "Enter your Aadhaar virtual ID or Aadhaar number"

**Q: How do I generate an Aadhaar Virtual ID (VID)?**
A Virtual ID is a temporary 16-digit number linked to your Aadhaar that you can share instead of your actual Aadhaar number. You can generate one through UIDAI's resident portal or by sending an SMS to UIDAI's registered number (the exact URL and SMS format are maintained by UIDAI and may change — use the "Generate Aadhaar Virtual ID" link shown on this screen). The VID is accepted in place of your full Aadhaar number.

**Q: I don't have Aadhaar**
Tap **I DON'T HAVE AADHAAR**. Per the welcome screen, opening a digiSavings account without Aadhaar requires a DBS branch visit; the digital eKYC flow requires Aadhaar.

**Q: What does DBS do with my Aadhaar number?**
The consent text on this screen reads verbatim: *"I voluntarily authorize DBS to use my Aadhaar details for eKYC authentication and getting my KYC details from UIDAI for account opening. I indemnify DBS against all losses arising due to difference in my name on Aadhaar and PAN card, if any."* DBS uses it only for identity verification with UIDAI.

## Step 9 — OTP Verification

**Q: I haven't received the OTP**
The OTP is sent by SMS from sender ID **JD-DBSBNK-S** to the mobile number you entered in Step 5 (which must be your Aadhaar-linked number). Check that the number entered was correct, and check your SMS inbox and spam folder. Wait for the 90-second timer to expire before tapping RESEND OTP.

**Q: The OTP has expired**
OTPs expire after a short window. Once the 90-second resend timer finishes, the RESEND OTP button becomes active — tap it to request a new OTP and enter that one instead.

**Q: The RESEND OTP button is greyed out**
The RESEND OTP button is intentionally disabled for **90 seconds** after an OTP is sent. Wait for the countdown timer to reach zero; the button will become active and you can request a fresh OTP.

**Q: Is the SMS from "JD-DBSBNK-S" legitimate?**
Yes. **JD-DBSBNK-S** is the registered SMS sender ID for DBS digibank OTP messages in India. An SMS from this sender asking for a 6-digit OTP during your account opening is expected and legitimate. Never share this OTP with anyone outside the app's OTP field — DBS will never ask for your OTP over a phone call or chat.

## Step 10 — "Residential address and preferred branch"

**Q: My Aadhaar address is outdated or wrong**
Your residential address is pulled from your Aadhaar records. Tap **CHANGE ADDRESS** to enter a different address. Note that the address you confirm here is where your debit card will be delivered, so make sure it is accurate.

**Q: Where will my debit card be delivered?**
The app states: *"Your debit card will also be delivered to this address."* — meaning the residential address shown on this screen. If that address is wrong, use CHANGE ADDRESS before proceeding.

**Q: How do I pick or change my preferred branch?**
Tap **CHANGE BRANCH** to open the branch selector. You can search by city and select the DBS branch most convenient for you.

## Step 11 — "What is your occupation?"

**Q: I'm a freelancer — which option should I choose?**
The app provides **Self-employed** or **Professional** as the closest matches. Self-employed is generally suitable for gig workers and freelancers without a registered profession; Professional is typically for qualified practitioners (doctors, lawyers, chartered accountants). Choose whichever best describes your primary work arrangement.

**Q: I'm between jobs / currently unemployed**
There is no "unemployed" option listed. If you are temporarily between jobs, consider whether "Service — Private/Public Sector" applies to your most recent work, or speak with a DBS banker at a branch who can guide you for your specific situation.

**Q: Why does DBS need my occupation?**
This is a KYC (Know Your Customer) requirement under RBI guidelines. Banks are required to understand the nature of their customers' economic activity as part of the account-opening process.

## Step 13 — "Your estimated financial assets?"

**Q: What counts as financial assets?**
Financial assets generally include savings and current account balances, fixed/recurring deposits, mutual funds, stocks and bonds, and insurance policies with a cash value. They typically exclude your primary home and personal-use vehicles. Use the in-app "what are financial asset?" help link on this screen for DBS's specific definition.

**Q: Do I need to provide proof of my financial assets?**
No documents are needed at this step — you select the applicable band. Document verification, if required, happens at the KYC stage.

**Q: Can I add or change a nominee later?**

## Step 16 — "Account Selection — Our recommendations"

**Q: Which account should I pick?**
The trade-off is between the required Monthly Average Balance (MAB) and the welcome benefits you unlock. A higher MAB means more welcome offers but also greater exposure if your balance dips below the threshold. The choice depends on how much you plan to keep in the account — we won't pick for you, but the key numbers are in the next screen (Step 17, fees summary).

**Q: What does MAB mean?**
MAB stands for Monthly Average Balance. It is calculated as the average of your daily closing balances over the month. The exact calculation method is governed by DBS's product terms — check the in-app Schedule of Charges for the precise formula.

**Q: What is digiPortfolio?**
digiPortfolio is DBS's ready-made basket of mutual funds, available to DBS Growth Plus and Growth Max account holders. For specific details about how it works, refer to the digibank app's digiPortfolio section — we don't have additional details to share here.

**Q: Can I switch to a different account type later?**
We don't have confirmed details on the account-switching process. Contact DBS customer care after your account is active to ask about upgrading or changing your account type.

## Step 17 — "Important note" — Fees Summary

**Q: What is the fall-below fee?**
If your Monthly Average Balance falls below **₹10,000**, DBS charges **6% of the shortfall, up to a maximum of ₹500**. This is stated verbatim on this screen.

**Q: When is the debit card fee waived?**
The DBS Visa Classic debit card carries an annual fee of **₹199**. This fee is waived if you meet **any one** of these three thresholds: yearly spending of **₹1,00,000 or more**, OR a Monthly Average Balance of **₹50,000 or more**, OR a Total Relationship Value (TRV) of **₹2,00,000 or more**.

**Q: What is TRV (Total Relationship Value)?**
The app defines it verbatim as: *"TRV is across all accounts in a family and calculated by aggregating average quarterly saving account balances and end of period term deposits and investments."*

**Q: Do I have to fund the account with ₹25,000 today?**
No. The screen title says: *"Fund your account with ₹25,000 in 30 days to avail maximum benefits."* You have a 30-day window from account opening to fund it and unlock the welcome benefits.

## Step 18 — "Key terms & conditions"

**Q: Where can I read the full Terms & Conditions?**
The full T&C are shown in-app at this step and are also available at: `https://www.dbs.com/digibank/in/terms-and-conditions.page`

**Q: What's the difference between an e-wallet and a savings account in the T&C?**
Per the T&C definitions: a **DBS Bank e-wallet** is a *semi-closed wallet product*. A **Savings Account** is a full bank account. The digiSavings account you're opening is a Savings Account, not an e-wallet.

## Step 19 — "Verify your identity — Choose an option"

**Q: What are the Video KYC hours?**
Video KYC is available:
- Monday–Friday: 9:00 am – 7:30 pm
- 1st, 3rd & 5th Saturday: 9:00 am – 7:30 pm
- 2nd & 4th Saturday: 9:00 am – 6:00 pm
- Closed on Sundays and bank holidays

**Q: What if I miss the Video KYC hours?**
You can either wait for the next available slot (the hours above), or switch to the branch visit path by selecting "Visit a branch" instead of the video call option.

**Q: What happens on the video call?**
A DBS agent will connect with you via video, verify your identity, and complete your KYC. The estimated time is approximately 5 minutes. We don't have the exact steps the agent follows — the agent will guide you through.

**Q: What do I need to bring to the branch?**
Bring your **original PAN card**, your **original Aadhaar card** (or a printout of your e-Aadhaar), and the **reference code** displayed on your screen in Step 20.

## Step 20 — Branch Visit Path

**Q: I lost my reference code**
Log back into the digibank app using your username and password from Step 6 — your application is saved and the reference code will be shown again when you return to the KYC step.

**Q: How long until my account is active?**
The app says: *"You will receive an SMS when your account is verified."* We cannot confirm a specific number of days — it depends on branch processing. Watch for that SMS.

**Q: Can I switch from branch verification to video KYC?**
Yes. On the branch confirmation screen (Step 20), there is a **CHANGE VERIFICATION METHOD** button that lets you switch to the video call option.

## Post-Flow Questions

**Q: When do I get my physical debit card?**
Your debit card — if you took the branch (biometric) path and are receiving a physical card — will be mailed to the residential address you confirmed at Step 10. We cannot confirm a specific delivery timeline; check with DBS customer care if it doesn't arrive.

**Q: When can I log in for the first time after opening my account?**
Once you receive the SMS confirming your account is verified (after KYC completion), you can log in to the digibank app using the Username and Password you set in Step 6.

**Q: I chose the digital path — will I get a virtual or physical debit card?**
Per the T&C definitions: a **virtual debit card** is issued for Savings Accounts opened via OTP-based eKYC (the fully digital path). A **physical debit card** is issued for Savings Accounts opened via biometric verification (the branch path). If you completed your account opening fully online, your card is virtual and accessible in the app.
